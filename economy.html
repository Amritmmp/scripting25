ECONOMY

The Food Basket of Industrial Hub

The backbone of Punjab's economy remains its highly developed *agricultural sector. Known worldwide as the *'Granary of India'**, the state leads the nation in the production of wheat, rice, and cotton, driven by extensive irrigation networks and advanced farming techniques. This robust agricultural output not only sustains the state but also contributes significantly to India's food security. The sector has led to the development of allied industries, including food processing and dairy farming, which are major economic drivers.Beyond farming, Punjab has a strong *manufacturing base, particularly in Ludhiana, which is famous for its bicycle and hosiery production, often called the 'Manchester of India'. Other key industries include the manufacturing of scientific instruments in Ambala and sports goods. In recent years, the government has focused on boosting **service sectors* like IT, education, and healthcare, seeking to diversify the economy and attract foreign investment, ensuring sustainable future growth

AGRICULTURE MANUFACTURING

Emerging Sectors

Punjab's economy is Agri-based. It is also referred to as India's "Grain House" since wheat and rice are cultivated here in huge amounts. Land here is extremely fertile; all other crops are cultivated here. Industrial cities such as Ludhiana are present there, which are industrial towns where clothes, machines, and other items are produced. IT and service industry training also have begun emerging recently. Punjab led all Indian states in GDP per capita in 1981 and was fourth in 2001 but had been growing more slowly than the rest of India in recent years and had the second-lowest GDP per capita growth rate among all Indian union territories and states from 2000 to 2010.
