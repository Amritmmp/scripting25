GEOGRAPHY

Fertile Plains, Rivers and Varied Climate

The state of Punjab is geographically defined by its vast, unbroken alluvial plains, making it one of the most fertile regions globally. Historically, the name Punjab signifies 'The Land of Five Rivers'—Beas, Sutlej, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum—though today, only the Beas, Sutlej, and Ravi flow exclusively through the state. This extensive network of rivers and canals is the lifeline of Punjab, supporting its massive agricultural output and earning it the title of the 'Granary of India'. The flat terrain is ideal for both farming and infrastructure development.Punjab experiences a semi-arid to sub-humid climate, characterized by scorching summers and cool, sometimes foggy, winters. The geography can be broadly divided into three regions: the Majha (the land between the Ravi and Beas), the Doaba (between the Beas and Sutlej), and the Malwa (south of the Sutlej). Each region boasts unique agricultural and cultural variations, tied directly to the soil quality and proximity to the rivers. This geographical diversity is what makes the state resilient and ecologically unique.

The Land of Five Rivers

Fertile Soil and Varied Climate

Punjab is in the northwestern part of India and is known as the land of five rivers. Its name is derived from the combination of Punj+ab because it is the land of five rivers. Rivers like Sutlej, Beas, and Ravi make this state fertile for agriculture. Punjab is bordered by Jammu and Kashmir to the north, Himachal Pradesh to the east, Haryana to the south and Pakistan to the west. Most of Punjab is plains, but some areas also have mild mountains. It experiences extreme heat in summer and severe cold in winter. Punjab is considered ideal for agriculture due to its natural resources and fertile soil.
