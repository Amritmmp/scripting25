ATTRACTIONS

Landmarks of Faith

Punjab is home to a magnificent array of attractions that cater to every traveller, ranging from spiritual sanctuaries to monumental symbols of history. The foremost destination is the Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple) in Amritsar, the spiritual and cultural heart of the Sikh faith, renowned for its stunning architecture and atmosphere of serenity. Nearby, Jallianwala Bagh serves as a profound and poignant memorial, commemorating a pivotal moment in India's fight for independence. These sites together form the bedrock of Punjab's identity.Beyond Amritsar, visitors flock to the Wagah Border near Attari to witness the intensely energetic and colourful "Beating Retreat" ceremony, a daily display of patriotic fervor. For those interested in royal history, the Qila Mubarak in Bathinda and Patiala offers a glimpse into the regional monarchies. Additionally, experiencing the authentic Punjabi lifestyle through a visit to a local village or farmstay provides a unique cultural immersion, making Punjab a destination of rich and varied experiences.

GOLDEN HEART OF INDIA

Landmarks of Faith

Punjab attracts millions of tourists every year. The Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) of Amritsar is famous all over the world and millions of tourists visit here every year. The daily ceremony of Wagah Border attracts people. Chandigarh’s Rock Garden, Jalandhar’s fairs and seasonal festivals of villages are the center of special interest for tourists. Punjabis food, such as mustard greens, corn bread and lassi, attracts everyone mind. Music, dance and folk rituals are identity of Punjab which are always remembered by tourists.
